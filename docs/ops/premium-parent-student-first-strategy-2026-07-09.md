# Premium Parent-Funded Strategy — 2026-07-09

## Decision

Tendo is now a **premium direct-to-parent / student-first study product**.

We are not building primarily for schools. We are building for parents who already invest heavily in education and want clear academic ROI for their children.

## Target customer

Primary target:

- Parents/guardians paying roughly **UGX 1.2M+ per school term** or similar education spend.
- Families who can afford about **UGX 50,000/month** for a serious study product.
- Parents who care about PLE performance, visibility, discipline, progress and structured home study.

Primary user:

- P6/P7 learners first, then systematically expand downward class by class.

Supporting users:

- Parents/guardians: pay, monitor, encourage, need ROI.
- Teachers/tutors: can set work and mark, but are not the main growth dependency.

## What this means

### We optimise for

- Premium feel.
- Content completeness and accuracy.
- Clear child progress.
- Parent confidence.
- Phone-first experience.
- Fast iteration.
- A product that can be sold directly without waiting for school approval.

### We stop optimising for

- School pilot dependency.
- School branding as a core feature.
- Overbuilding teacher dashboards before parent dashboards.
- Anything that makes the app look like an unfinished internal demo.

## Pricing assumption

Working target:

- **UGX 50,000/month** per learner/family as a premium study subscription.

This price only works if the app feels premium and visibly useful:

- parent can see usage
- parent can see weaknesses
- parent receives recommendations
- child has complete subject coverage
- content feels trustworthy

## Current product priorities

1. Finish and audit **Primary 7** content to premium standard.
2. Move to **Primary 6**, then the next class, systematically.
3. Build parent dashboard/ROI view after content foundation is stable enough.
4. Keep teacher tools useful but not central.
5. Add backend auth when needed for parent/student sync.

## Product principle

> Premium parents pay when they can see serious study, clear progress and a path to better results.

The product must feel alive, complete, accurate and worth paying for.
