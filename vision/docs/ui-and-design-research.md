# UI & Design Research (P4-P7 Gaming Focus)

To meet the psychological needs of 9-13 year old Ugandan students, the UI must completely distance itself from early childhood design patterns. It must look and feel like a modern, competitive gaming interface (e.g., Kahoot, Prodigy, or Fortnite UI).

## 1. Aesthetic Direction: Sleek, Dark, and Intense
- **Base Palette:** Deep blacks, dark slates (`#0F172A`, `#1E293B`) to provide a mature canvas.
- **Accent Colors:** Neon/Cyberpunk accents for primary actions and highlights (Electric Cyan, Neon Purple, Hyper Green). This screams "gaming" rather than "school".
- **Typography:** Bold, geometric sans-serifs (like `Orbitron`, `Russo One`, or ultra-bold `Inter`) for headers and ranks. Highly legible, clean sans-serifs for body text to support reading comprehension.

## 2. Competitive UI Elements
- **The Rank Badge:** Central to the dashboard. A beautifully designed shield or emblem representing their current status (e.g., a glowing Gold badge for "Division Two", shifting to a radiant Diamond badge for "Division One Master").
- **The Leaderboard:** A persistent side-panel or prominent tab showing live rankings. Avatars are displayed prominently next to scores.

## 3. Interactive Subject "Worlds"
- **Science / SST:** Instead of a list of chapters, present an interactive Map. For SST, it's a map of East Africa. For Science, it could be a map of the human body or a planetary system.
- **Tactile Inputs:** Replace radio buttons with drag-and-drop mechanics or tap-to-reveal hotspots on the maps.

## 4. Offline-First Indicators (Ugandan Infrastructure Reality)
- **The Problem:** Data is expensive, and internet drops are frequent.
- **The Solution:** A persistent top-bar indicator showing connection status. 
- **Offline UI:** Content that is cached offline has a solid neon-green outline or icon. When offline, inaccessible content is grayed out gracefully rather than showing a broken error page.
