# Metrics & Success Tracking (P4-P7 Gaming Focus)

For the pre-teen Ugandan market, our metrics must capture not just raw engagement, but the specific competitive and infrastructural drivers we are building for.

## 1. Competitive Engagement Metrics (The "Gamer" Hook)
- **Leaderboard Interactions:** How often does a student check the leaderboard panel? High interaction indicates the competitive mechanics are working.
- **Rank Up Velocity:** How fast are students moving from "Bronze" to "Silver"? If this is too fast, the prestige is lost. If too slow, they churn.
- **Avatar/Gear Customization Events:** Are they spending their earned XP/Coins to customize their profile? This proves they care about their social status in the app.

## 2. Infrastructure Resilience Metrics (The Ugandan Reality)
- **Offline Session Ratio:** What percentage of study sessions happen entirely offline? This validates the necessity of the offline-first architecture.
- **Sync Success Rate:** When a device reconnects, what is the success rate of syncing offline progress to the server? A failure here destroys the child's motivation (lost XP).
- **Time-to-Interactive (TTI) on 3G:** The app must load its core dashboard in under 3 seconds even on a weak mobile connection.

## 3. Academic Efficacy (The Parent ROI)
- **PLE Simulation Accuracy:** Does their "Division One" projected rank in the app correlate with their actual school midterm results? 
- **Knowledge Retention on Retry:** When a student gets an answer wrong and is presented with a "Dual-Coded" visual card, what is their success rate on the next attempt? This proves our UX reduces cognitive load effectively.
