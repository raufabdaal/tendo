# Learning Psychology & Engagement Mechanics (P4-P7 Focus)

The target demographic for Tendo is 9-13 year olds preparing for the Primary Leaving Examinations (PLE). This changes the psychological drivers completely from early childhood gamification to pre-teen social status and high-stakes performance.

## 1. The Psychological Shift: "Don't Make Me Look Like a Baby"
- **The Core Driver:** Pre-teens crave autonomy and maturity. They reject UI that feels like it belongs in lower primary.
- **Implementation:**
  - Shift from primary colors to a dark mode, "hacker/gamer" aesthetic.
  - Replace "streaks" with "Ranks" (e.g., Bronze, Silver, Gold, Division One Master).
  - Use avatars that can be customized with "cool" gear unlocked via studying, similar to Prodigy Math or Roblox.

## 2. Leveraging PLE Pressure (Anxiety into Motivation)
- **The Core Driver:** The entire Ugandan educational ecosystem focuses on achieving a "Division One" in P7. We must channel this external pressure into internal motivation.
- **Implementation:**
  - **Division One Status Symbol:** A prominent badge on the dashboard that represents their projected PLE performance.
  - **School & Peer Leaderboards:** A competitive ranking system where they can see how they stack up against classmates or national peers, tapping into their intense need for social proof and peer status.

## 3. Dual-Coding & Cognitive Load Management
- **The Core Driver:** P4 is the transition year to English as the primary medium of instruction. Huge walls of text will cause immediate disengagement due to cognitive overload.
- **Implementation:**
  - Break comprehension passages and science concepts into swipeable, bite-sized cards.
  - Every text explanation must be paired with a high-quality icon or interactive visual (Dual-Coding Theory).

## 4. Subjects as "Worlds"
- **The Core Driver:** Traditional subjects feel like work. "Campaigns" feel like a challenge to be conquered.
- **Implementation:**
  - Math, English, Science, and SST are presented as massive worlds to explore.
  - Completing topics unlocks new sectors of the map, providing a tangible sense of progression.
